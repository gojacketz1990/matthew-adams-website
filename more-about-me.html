Hello, I am Matthew Adams

As an ISTQB CTFL certified Quality Assurance Lead and Automation Engineer from Marietta, Georgia I focus on embedding quality at every stage in the lifecycle. I specialize in providing subject matter expertise, optimizing quality workflows and building robust automation frameworks, My passion lies in designing meticulous testing strategies that minimize defects and optimize performance. Explore my approach to quality and connect with me.

My background also includes extensive experience in performance testing enterprise applications, capacity planning and performance tuning across diverse architectures.

I went to school to be a Mechanical Engineer and worked in my field for a while designing refrigerator handles which consisted of drawing using AutoCAD for about seven hours a day. I had very little interaction with anyone else and it became monotonous and boring very quickly.

Myself and a few buddies were out having a couple drinks on a Friday night and one of my friends mentioned that he had just started a software company. He said I would be perfect for a QA Engineering position that they had open but you don't really have any experience. I had done some QA in the manufactoring industry in summers and having a firm grasp on engineering concepts I said to him "engineering is engineering". I started three weeks later and never looked back.

Over the next couple years I steered more towards the performance side of QA and did performance testing and capacity planning almost exclusively for a few years. I could tune WebLogic, WebSphere and Apache Web Servers with the best of them. Through companies going out of business I ended up at Turner Broadcasting as a contractor for 18 months doing performance testing (LoadRunner, Performance Center) on internal applications. I left TBS for a year since the contractor time limit had been reached and came back a year later as a full time employee

For the next 16 years we went through quite a lot of change at TBS. We became Time Warner, then AT+T and then Warner Brothers Discovery.
